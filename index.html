I am a software engineer. I am passionate about computing, mathematics, philosophy and music.

My Story

I am a senior at Pomona College in Claremont, California studying Mathematics & Statistics with a minor in Philosophy. Originally, I am from Mamaroneck New York. I came to Pomona with the intention of studying physics, but decided to pursue mathematics after taking Linear Algebra during my first semester, where I first came to appreciate the beauty and abstraction of mathematical reasoning.

Throughout my academic journey, I have also harbored an admiration for computer science, which I have expressed through coursework, personal projects, and internships. I am fascinated by the breadth of areas in which computers are extraordinarily powerful tools, which, among my personal interests, spans financial technology, social activism, mathematics research, privacy and security, and more.

Outside of technology, I am passionate about music and philosophy. In middle school, I started learning to play jazz and it has been a passion ever since. Here in Claremont, I spend much of my time gigging, playing for fun with friends, and trying to support the campus band scene through the Pomona College Musicians' Coalition.

Currently, I am focused on making the most of my last year on campus, writing my thesis on judgment aggregation, and searching for post-graduate opportunities. I am interested in technology roles which will allow me to leverage my mathematics background, particularly in financial technology.
